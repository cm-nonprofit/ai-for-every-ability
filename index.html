The problem

The system is hard to read — for the families who need it most.

One in six children in the United States has a developmental disability. Their families spend months — sometimes years — searching for therapists, navigating IEPs, decoding insurance, and pleading for waitlists to open. The answers exist. They are just not connected.

"I had four binders, six tabs open, and a child who needed me to be present. The information was everywhere, but the support wasn't." — Marisol, parent of an autistic 7-year-old

What gets in the way

Decisions about therapy, schooling, and accommodations are made under pressure, with fragmented information, and often without anyone who has walked the same road. We built AI for Every Ability to change that.

About us

How a student project became a movement.

AI for Every Ability started as a high school computer science project — a simple chatbot that helped one family find local autism resources. Today, it is a nonprofit serving thousands of families with AI-powered tools built alongside the disability community.

Phase 01 — 2023

A chatbot for one family

Cheng built a prototype to help a neighbor's family find a Mandarin-speaking ABA therapist in 90 minutes — a search that had taken them three weeks.

Phase 02 — 2024

From prototype to platform

Partnering with two school districts and a parent advisory board, the prototype grew into a resource graph spanning 12,000 services across five states.

Phase 03 — 2025

A nonprofit, by design

We formalized as a nonprofit so that the people we serve — not investors — set the roadmap. Families, educators, and self-advocates sit on every product decision.

Programs

Five tools, one promise: nobody navigates alone.

Each program is co-designed with families, educators, and self-advocates, then tested in real classrooms and clinics before it reaches you.

PathFinder AI

Resource Navigator

A free, multilingual AI assistant that helps families find therapists, school programs, and waiver services within minutes — by ZIP code, language, and insurance.

ShiftSpeak

Communication Toolkit

Open-source AAC boards, sign-language translation, and plain-language IEP summarizers built with speech-language pathologists and Deaf advocates.

HiveSense

Family Mentor Network

A matching platform that pairs new families with experienced caregiver mentors in their district — people who have already walked the road they are starting.

EmberSpark

Sensory-Friendly Learning

Adaptive learning modules that auto-adjust pace, color, and sound based on real-time engagement signals — tested across 38 partner classrooms.

WaveAlert

Community Accessibility Map

A crowdsourced, AI-verified map of physical and sensory accessibility — restrooms, ramps, quiet rooms — built by and for the disability community.

In development

RootKnow Insights

An aggregated, anonymized data dashboard that surfaces what is working — and what is not — so school districts and policymakers can fund the right interventions.
